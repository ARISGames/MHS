I've created a test game called 'MHS_Iron_Mine_Test'. You should be able to search to find it on the ARIS client (Make sure that 'show games in development' is marked 'YES' in ARIS settings). The game has 6 web pages that are spread out randomly throughout Minnesota/Wisconsin. They are all quick-travel-able, so just clicking on them should get you right to them. Each game has a placeholder screen for an Intro, and a descriptive video. Click anywhere to go to the next screen. Once you are on the 'gameplay' screen for any game, you will see two indicators of money- the first (prefixed with a '+') is how much you currently have, and the second is how much you 'need'. Right now, all 'needs' are set to $0.00, but are ready to be swapped out with meaningful variables when ready.

*NOTE*- These games are ugly. Like, really, really ugly. And they do an awful job illustrating what is going on. They are built to be easily modifiable and to allow for easy switching out/ positioning/ styling of assets. So please don't let that distract you. :)
